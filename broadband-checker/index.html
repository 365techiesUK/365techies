// WHO SERVES DORSET

Your broadband options, honestly mapped

Who you can actually get depends on your exact street — here’s the real Dorset landscape.

  • National providers (everywhere)

    BT, Sky, TalkTalk, EE, Vodafone, Plusnet, Zen & NOW — on the Openreach network across Dorset, as full fibre (FTTP) or older part-fibre (FTTC) depending on your line.

  • Virgin Media (urban cable)

    Its own cable network covers most of urban Bournemouth, Christchurch & Poole with gigabit speeds — little rural coverage. Check your exact postcode.

  • Full-fibre altnets (BCP)

    toob and others on the CityFibre network bring symmetrical full fibre to parts of BCP — availability varies street by street.

  • Wessex Internet (rural Dorset)

    Dorset-based and rural-focused — full fibre and fixed wireless reaching villages the big networks don’t, including Project Gigabit areas.

  • Dorset towns full fibre

    Weymouth, Bridport, Sherborne & Dorchester have ex-Jurassic full fibre (reopening to new orders via Brillband from mid-2026) and growing Openreach FTTP.

  • New-build estates

    Newer developments (e.g. parts of Gillingham) are often on dedicated full-fibre networks — we’ll check if yours is.

Slow old lines can now cost more than full fibre (Ofcom, 2025) — so switching up can genuinely save money as well as speed.

// SWITCH SMART

What to watch when you switch

  • Out of contract = overpaying

    Once your deal ends, the price usually jumps. If you’re past your minimum term, you can almost always do better.

  • ‘From’ prices are intro rates

    Headline deals are introductory and rise mid-contract — check what you’ll pay over the whole term, not just month one.

  • Mid-contract price rises

    Most big providers raise prices each spring (often around £3–4/month). A few, like Zen, don’t — worth comparing like for like.

  • Full fibre vs part fibre

    ‘Fibre’ isn’t always full fibre. FTTP (to your home) is faster and more reliable than older FTTC — check which you’d get.

  • One Touch Switch

    Switching is easier now — your new provider sorts it and cancels the old line. No double-billing, minimal downtime.

  • Keep your email & Wi-Fi working

    The bit people forget — we make sure your email, devices and Wi-Fi all keep working after a switch.

// GOOD QUESTIONS

Frequently asked

Which broadband providers are available in Dorset?

Across most of Dorset you can get the national ISPs on the Openreach network — BT, Sky, TalkTalk, EE, Vodafone, Plusnet, Zen and NOW. In urban Bournemouth, Christchurch & Poole you’ll often also have Virgin Media cable and full-fibre altnets like toob/CityFibre. In rural Dorset, the Dorset-based Wessex Internet fills many of the gaps. Exact availability is postcode-specific, so always check your address.

Can you tell me the exact price for my address?

Broadband prices are address-specific and change almost weekly, with introductory deals that rise mid-contract — so we don’t quote a figure we can’t stand behind. Use the official/live checkers we link to for today’s prices, or let us do a free impartial review for your exact postcode.

Is switching broadband a hassle?

Not any more — under ‘One Touch Switch’ your new provider arranges the switch and cancels the old line for you. We can guide you through it and make sure your Wi-Fi and email keep working.

I’m out of contract — am I overpaying?

Very possibly. Out-of-contract customers usually pay more than new-customer prices, and old slow lines can now cost more than full fibre. It’s well worth a check.

Do you sell broadband?

We’re independent — we help you find and switch to the best deal for your address, then make sure it’s all set up and your Wi-Fi works properly. Honest advice, no commission-chasing.
