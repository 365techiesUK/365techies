// GOOD QUESTIONS

Frequently asked

What does the link safety checker do?

It examines a web address for the tell-tale signs of a phishing or scam link — things like a missing secure connection, an IP address instead of a domain, disguised characters, or a shortened link that hides where it really goes — and explains what it finds in plain English.

Does it open the link?

No — and that’s deliberate. We only read and analyse the address in your browser, so it’s completely safe to check a link you’re unsure about.

If it says ‘no red flags’, is the link definitely safe?

It’s reassuring, but not a guarantee — a clean-looking address can still be malicious. Always ask yourself: were you expecting this link? If a message feels off, don’t click — check with us.

I’ve clicked a bad link — what now?

Don’t panic. Don’t enter any details, close the page, and if you already typed a password, change it straight away. See our I’ve been scammed guide, or call us — we’ll help.

Where can I learn to spot scams myself?

Try our free Spot the Scam quiz and our online safety hub — both in plain English, no jargon.

Had a suspicious message?

Forward it, screenshot it, or just describe it — we’ll tell you if it’s a scam and what to do, with no judgement. Helping Dorset stay safe online since 1995.
