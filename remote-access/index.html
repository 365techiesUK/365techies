// THE OLD WAY vs WORKING REMOTELY

The difference it makes, side by side

Without remote access (the old way)

  • Drive in for anything you forgot — even a single file
  • Email files back and forth to yourself, and lose track of the latest version
  • Buy, licence and update a second computer to mirror your work setup
  • Stuck and unproductive at home if you’re ill, snowed in or can’t travel
  • A commute each way, every day — fuel, time and carbon
  • Sensitive files copied onto USB sticks and personal laptops

With remote access (the modern way)

  • Open any device and you’re on your actual work PC in seconds
  • One set of files and software — always the latest, nothing duplicated
  • No second machine to buy, licence or keep updated
  • Work straight through illness, weather or travel disruption
  • Skip the commute when you can — save the time, fuel and carbon
  • Files stay on the host PC; only an encrypted picture of the screen travels

// A TALE OF TWO MONDAYS

The same morning, two different weeks

Here’s the kind of difference it makes for a typical small business or home worker in Dorset.

Monday, the old way

You wake up streaming with a cold. The quote a client needs today is on the office PC — so it’ll have to wait, or you drag yourself in and infect the team. Half your morning goes on the round trip. The file you actually needed turns out to be on the other machine. By the time you’re set up at home with a half-remembered copy, it’s lunchtime and the client has gone quiet.

Monday, with remote access

You wake up streaming with a cold. You open the laptop in bed, log in, and you’re on your office PC — the real one, with the quote already open where you left it on Friday. You finish it, send it before nine, and the client replies by ten. No commute, no second machine, no infecting the team, no lost day. You rest up in the afternoon, and the work still got done.

An illustration, not a specific customer — but it’s the everyday difference our customers tell us about. More on the green side on our sustainability page.

// HOW IT WORKS

Set up properly, in three steps

  1. 01

    We set it up

  2. 02

// GOOD QUESTIONS

Frequently asked

What’s the difference between this and your remote support?

Two different things, and we set up both. Remote support is when we securely connect to your computer to fix a problem while you watch. Remote access is when you connect to your own computer — your work PC from home, say — to get on with your day. This page is about the second one.

Is it secure?

Yes. Splashtop Business protects every session with TLS and 256-bit AES encryption, device authentication and two-step login, so only you get in. Crucially, your files never leave the host computer — only an encrypted picture of the screen travels — so nothing sensitive ends up on the device you’re sitting at.

Will it be fast enough for real work?

Yes. It’s smooth and responsive even with large files and design work — most people quickly forget they’re not sat at the machine. Real-world speed depends on the internet connection at both ends, which we’ll check and help you get right.

Can I connect from a phone, tablet or someone else’s laptop?

Yes — you can connect from almost any device (laptop, tablet or phone) to your own Windows computer, using the free Splashtop app. When you log off, nothing is left behind on the device you borrowed.

Does my computer have to be left switched on?

The computer you’re reaching does need to be on, or set to wake up on demand — we’ll configure that for you so it’s ready when you need it, on the lowest sensible power so it isn’t wasting energy.

How much does it cost?

It’s a small, sensible add-on that we set up, secure and look after for you. We’ll quote it alongside your support plan — just ask us and we’ll keep it simple.
