// CONFIDENCE GUIDE

Safe online banking for beginners

Online banking is genuinely safe when you follow a few simple habits — and it can make life much easier. Here’s how to bank online with confidence, written for people who’d rather be careful.

// THE GOLDEN RULES

Four habits that keep you safe

Your bank will never ask you to move money

No genuine bank, or the police, will ever ask you to move money to a ‘safe account’ or read out a full passcode. That’s always a scam. This is the big one to remember.

Always go to the bank yourself

Open your banking app, or type the bank’s address in yourself. Never log in through a link in a text or email.

Use your own connection

Bank on your home Wi-Fi or mobile data — not public Wi-Fi in a café or library.

Lock your devices

A PIN, fingerprint or face unlock on your phone and computer keeps your banking private if they’re lost.

Getting set up safely

  • Download your bank’s official app from the official app store (or type the bank’s web address yourself).
  • Use a strong, unique password that’s different from your email — try our password checker.
  • Turn on the bank’s app login with your fingerprint or face, and any two-step verification — see our 2FA guide.

Spotting a banking scam

Be on guard if you get an unexpected call, text or email that:

  • creates panic or urgency (‘your account is at risk, act now’);
  • asks you to move money, share a passcode, or install software;
  • contains a link to ‘log in’ or ‘verify’ your details.

See our pages on scam texts & calls and try the Spot the Scam quiz.

If something feels wrong

Stop. Hang up or close the message. Then contact your bank yourself using 159 or the number on the back of your card. Forward scam texts to 7726 and report fraud to Action Fraud on 0300 123 2040. If money has gone, follow our guide on what to do if you’ve been scammed.

Trusted free advice: Take Five to Stop Fraud and the FCA’s ScamSmart. This is general IT and security guidance, not financial advice — always use your bank’s official app or website.

// GOOD QUESTIONS

Frequently asked

Is online banking actually safe?

Yes — banks invest heavily in security, and following a few simple habits makes it very safe. The biggest risk isn’t the bank being hacked; it’s being tricked into handing details over, which the golden rules above prevent.

Will my bank ever phone and ask me to move money?

Never. No genuine bank or police officer will ask you to move money to a ‘safe account’ or read out a full code. If someone does, it’s a scam — hang up and call 159.

Is it safe to bank on my phone?

Yes — the official banking app is one of the safest ways to bank, as long as your phone has a screen lock and you keep it updated.

Can you help me get set up?

Of course — we’ll help you install your banking app safely and set up secure login, patiently and at your pace. Just get in touch.

Want a friendly hand getting started?

We’ll help you set up online banking safely and confidently — and you’ll always have a real person to ask ‘is this safe?’
