## 3. Stolen-laptop tip sheet — send this if a journalist bites Pitch ④
A ready expert piece: *"What to do if your laptop or phone is stolen — and how to make it recoverable first."* (Owner: tweak the quote to sound like you.)

**Before it happens (5 minutes that change everything):**
1. Turn on **Find My Device** — Windows (Microsoft account) and **Find My Device** on Android.
2. Switch on **device encryption** (BitLocker on Windows) so a thief can't read your files.
3. Set a **strong login + automatic lock** after a few minutes idle.
4. Keep a **current backup** (cloud or an offline drive) so a loss is an inconvenience, not a disaster.
5. **Write down the serial number** (and photograph the receipt) — police and insurers ask for it.

**In the first hour after it's stolen:**
1. Use **Find My Device** to locate, lock and, if needed, **erase** it remotely.
2. **Change your passwords** — email first (it's the key to everything), then banking and shopping.
3. Turn on **two-factor authentication** if it isn't already.
4. **Report it to the police** and get a **crime reference number** (needed for insurance).
5. **Tell your bank** and your IT support — quick action limits the damage.

**Can you actually get it back?** Sometimes, yes. A stolen device often **reveals its location when it next connects to the internet** — which is exactly how we helped Dorset Police recover a customer's laptop. The golden rule: **let the police act on it — never confront a thief yourself.**

> **Suggested quote (adjust freely):** "Most people's instinct is to panic about the hardware,
> but the real value is the data and the accounts behind it. Lock it, change your email password,
> and report it — do those three things in the first hour and you've contained 90% of the risk.
> And set it up to be findable *before* anything happens; that's what gets devices back."
> — [Your name], 365 Techies
