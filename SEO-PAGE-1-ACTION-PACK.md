# 365 Techies — Page-1 Action Pack (the off-page playbook)

**The honest situation:** your website is already the strongest of any local IT-support
rival (it ranked #1 of 11 on on-page strength). For competitive Google terms, page 1 now
comes from three things the *website alone* can't buy: **authority (backlinks)**, **local
signals (Google Business Profile, reviews, citations)**, and **time**. The on-page side is
done and maintained by me — **this pack is the off-page work, which only you can execute.**

Do them roughly in this order. Items 1–3 are this-week wins; 4–5 are the real long-term lever.

---

## 1. Reviews engine — your fastest local win
Reviews are a top Google "map pack" ranking factor, and you've got a great base (4.9 / 51+).
Make it a steady trickle, not a one-off burst.

- Get your review link: **Google Business Profile → "Ask for reviews" → copy link** (looks like `g.page/r/…`).
- **Text every happy customer** after the job (you have the SMS number 07520 615332):
  > Hi [name], thanks for choosing 365 Techies today! If we did a good job, a quick Google
  > review would really help a local family firm like ours 🙏 [REVIEW LINK] — thank you, [name]
- Email version:
  > Subject: A quick favour? · Thanks again from 365 Techies
  > Hi [name], it was a pleasure helping with [job] today. If you were happy, a short Google
  > review makes a real difference to a family business that's been here since 1995: [REVIEW LINK].
  > Either way, we're always a call away on 01202 775566. — [name], 365 Techies
- **Reply to every review** (good and bad) — replies are themselves a ranking + trust signal.
- Aim for **2–4 new reviews a week**, steadily.

## 2. Google Business Profile — optimise fully
- **Primary category:** "Computer support and services". Add secondaries (Computer repair service, Computer consultant, Computer networking centre).
- **Services:** list every service with a short description (and prices where you publish them).
- **Description:** work in "IT support Bournemouth/Poole/Dorset, homes & businesses, since 1995".
- **Photos:** add regularly — team, the off-grid van, heritage shop, jobs in progress. Fresh photos signal an active business.
- **Posts:** publish weekly (a tip, an offer, the off-grid van, a new guide). Free and underused by rivals.
- **Q&A:** seed the common questions and answer them yourself.
- Keep **NAP** (name/address/phone) byte-identical to the website.

## 4. Local PR for backlinks — your #1 lever (this is what unlocks the hard terms)
Your weak/nofollow backlink profile is exactly why "IT support Bournemouth/Poole" stalls. You
can't pay your way up — you **earn editorial links** with stories. And you have genuinely
newsworthy ones no rival has. Pitch these (find the current newsdesk email on each outlet's
*Contact* page — don't guess it):

**Targets:** Bournemouth Echo (newsdesk), Dorset Biz News, Dorset Chamber, Bournemouth/Poole
community + business blogs, regional IT/trade press.

**Pitch A — "30 years of keeping Dorset connected" (heritage angle)**
> Family-run 365 Techies marks 30 years (since 1995). From running Dorset's Microsoft
> Education Resource Centre and supporting Mercedes-Benz Pentagon's garages across the south
> coast, to a solar-powered mobile support van today — a local-business-survival story.
> Offer: interview + photos (old shop, the van). Link: /about/

**Pitch B — "The IT firm that runs on sunshine"**
> 365 Techies' support "centre" is an off-grid solar campervan (the 365 Crafter) with a
> *live public dashboard* of its solar and battery — practising the clean-energy setups they
> install for Dorset homes and businesses. Sustainable Dorset member. Strong green/innovation
> hook for local + tech press. Link: /off-grid-victron-energy/

**Pitch C — "Helping the Environment Agency monitor the Avon's salmon"**
> In 2015, 365 Techies designed and still support a wireless data link across the River Avon
> at Knapp Mill, Christchurch, carrying the Environment Agency's fish-counter data. Conservation
> + local-tech angle. Link: /rural-and-farm-wifi-dorset/ (and knappmill.co.uk for the counter)

**Pitch D — follow-up to the 2011 stolen-laptop recovery**
> The Bournemouth Echo already covered how 365 helped Dorset Police recover a stolen laptop via
> remote-support software. Re-pitch a "how to protect yourself / what to do if it happens"
> piece tied to that story. Link: /lost-or-stolen-laptop-what-to-do/

Keep each pitch to a 2–3 sentence hook + "happy to provide photos / an interview" + the link.
